Graduate Research Assistant in Department of Oral Biology

08/2016 - 08/2021    University at Buffalo

Elucidated the mechanism of heparan sulfate (HS) inhibition of the collagenase activity of cathepsin K by the following approaches: 1) Using various biochemical methods to study cathepsin K and HS interactions and demonstrating how HS affects the activity and stability of cathepsin K in vitro. 2) Exploring cathepsin K-HS interactions in vivo by generating a cathepsin K knock-in mouse model. 3) Developing Cathepsin K antibodies that target the HS-binding site as potential therapeutic agents to treat bone diseases. The results of this study formed the basis of an R01 proposal that my PhD advisor submitted

Established a novel mechanism of pH-sensitive protein-HS interactions by showing a protein can incorporate two regions into one HS-binding site, leading to one first-author publication and one research grant

Demonstrated for the first time that a protein can be engineered to become a HS-binding protein, resulting in one first-author publication
